# AT2PO: Agentic Turn-based Policy Optimization via Tree Search
**arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.04767  
**Authors:** Zefang Zong et al. (Tencent Inc.)  
**Score:** 8/10

## Summary
AT2PO is a unified reinforcement learning framework for training multi-turn agentic LLMs that tackles three simultaneous failure modes in existing approaches: insufficient exploration diversity during rollout, sparse reward signals that cannot be attributed to individual turns, and policy updates that operate at the wrong granularity (sequence level rather than turn level). The framework introduces a turn-level tree structure that enables entropy-guided branching at uncertain decision points and backward reward propagation through the tree, paired with a novel turn-level policy optimization objective (ATPO) that applies importance-sampling and clipping per turn rather than per full trajectory. Evaluated across seven QA benchmarks on three backbone models (Qwen3-4B, Qwen3-8B, Qwen2.5-7B), AT2PO consistently outperforms state-of-the-art agentic RL baselines by up to 1.84 percentage points on average.

## Key Contributions
- **Turn-level tree structure for rollout:** Represents multi-turn agent interactions as a tree where each node is a turn, enabling branched exploration without blowing up the inference budget.
- **Entropy-Guided Tree Expansion (EGTE):** Uses the policy entropy at each turn to decide where to branch — high-entropy (uncertain) turns receive more children, focusing exploration budget on genuinely ambiguous decision points rather than expanding uniformly.
- **Turn-wise Credit Assignment (TCA):** Propagates sparse terminal rewards backward through the tree to assign fine-grained per-turn advantage estimates, replacing the standard practice of assigning the full sequence reward to every token.
- **Agentic Turn-based Policy Optimization (ATPO) objective:** Reformulates the GRPO/PPO clipping and importance-sampling ratio at the turn level rather than the token or sequence level, aligning gradient updates with the natural decision granularity of agentic tool-use.
- **Empirical validation at multiple scales:** Demonstrates consistent improvements over AEPO and DAPO baselines across four multi-hop QA datasets (HotpotQA, 2WikiMultiHopQA, MuSiQue, Bamboogle) and three single-hop QA datasets (NaturalQuestions, TriviaQA, PopQA).

## Methodology
**Tree construction:** During rollout, AT2PO builds a turn-level tree rooted at the initial context. At each turn, the agent generates candidate actions; the model's output entropy over the action distribution determines how many branches to spawn (high entropy → more branches). This replaces flat, independent rollouts (as in GRPO) with a structured tree that shares prefixes across branches, reducing redundant computation.

**Turn-wise Credit Assignment:** After rollout, terminal rewards (sparse, end-of-task signals) are propagated backward through the tree. Each turn node receives a value estimate derived from the downstream outcomes of its subtree, which is used to compute per-turn advantage estimates. This avoids the credit-diffusion problem where a single final reward is naively attributed to all turns equally.

**ATPO loss:** The policy optimization objective redefines the clipped surrogate loss so that the importance ratio `r_t = π_θ(a_t | s_t) / π_old(a_t | s_t)` is computed at the **turn level** (over the full turn's token sequence) rather than at the individual token level. Clipping and advantage weighting are applied turn-wise:

```
L_ATPO = E_t [ min(r_t · A_t,  clip(r_t, 1-ε, 1+ε) · A_t) ]
```

where `r_t` is the turn-level probability ratio, `A_t` is the turn-level advantage from TCA, and `ε` is the PPO clip coefficient. This mirrors how multi-turn agents actually make decisions (one turn at a time) rather than optimizing at the token level where credit is maximally diffuse.

**Training setup:** Built on top of the AEPO codebase; uses RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) server for tool-use grounding; supports Qwen3-4B, Qwen3-8B, and Qwen2.5-7B backbones with Flash-Attention.

## Results
Across all seven benchmarks and three backbone models, AT2PO outperforms the best reported baselines (AEPO, DAPO):

- **Multi-hop QA (Qwen3-8B):** AT2PO average 50.15% vs. AEPO 48.62% — a 1.53 pp gain.
- **Single-hop QA (Qwen3-8B):** AT2PO average 58.82% vs. DAPO 58.53% — a 0.29 pp gain.
- **Peak gain:** Up to 1.84 pp improvement in average across benchmarks (exact configuration not specified in available sources, likely smaller model where gains are largest).
- **Ablation finding:** The turn-level importance sampling and clipping (ATPO objective alone) is the single largest contributor to the improvement over vanilla GRPO; adding TCA on top provides further incremental gains; EGTE contributes additional exploration diversity especially on multi-hop tasks where tool choice is most uncertain.

## Relation to Forward-LM Research
**MCTS lookahead analog:** AT2PO's entropy-guided tree expansion is structurally analogous to the MCTS lookahead that is central to the forward-LM project. Both use tree-structured search to explore future trajectories rather than committing greedily. The key difference is domain: AT2PO is applied to tool-use QA agents (explore which retrieval action to take), while forward-LM targets dialogue policy (explore whether to Clarify, Commit, or Repair). The EGTE mechanism — branch more aggressively where the policy is uncertain — maps directly onto the forward-LM intuition that **high-entropy turns are precisely when lookahead planning is most valuable**.

**Turn-level credit assignment as solution to error propagation:** The TCA component directly addresses the forward-LM project's "error propagation across turns" problem. In dialogue, a bad Clarify/Commit choice at turn 2 degrades all subsequent turns; TCA provides a mechanism to assign blame to that specific turn from the sparse terminal outcome, which is exactly what would be needed to train a repair-aware dialogue policy via RL.

**Entropy as uncertainty signal:** The use of policy entropy to drive branching decisions is a concrete instantiation of the "variance across rollout trajectories as an informative signal" theme in the forward-LM context file. This provides a precedent and a reusable implementation pattern: entropy at a given turn can serve as a proxy for the VoI of taking a Clarify action at that turn.

**Positioning vs. CollabLLM and FLARE:** AT2PO occupies a similar niche to FLARE (Wang 2026) in using tree search for multi-turn LLM optimization, but differs in: (a) using entropy rather than receding-horizon replanning to guide branching, and (b) operating in a tool-use (retrieval) setting rather than open-domain dialogue. The forward-LM project can position itself as extending the AT2PO/FLARE line to **deliberate dialogue action selection** with typed intent operators (Clarify/Commit/Repair), rather than tool-call sequences.

**ATPO loss as a DPO/RL training technique:** The turn-level importance sampling objective is directly applicable to training the forward-LM's dialogue policy. If contrastive self-training (DPO) is replaced or augmented with online RL, ATPO provides a principled turn-level objective that avoids the known instability of token-level PPO for multi-turn tasks.

## Positioning
Cite as a state-of-the-art multi-turn agentic RL method that demonstrates the value of turn-level credit assignment and entropy-guided tree search in tool-use settings. Use as a baseline or methodological precedent for the forward-LM project's RL training component. Position the forward-LM contribution as extending AT2PO's turn-level RL insights from tool-use retrieval agents to **deliberate dialogue planning** with explicit uncertainty quantification and typed action categories (Clarify/Commit/Repair). The forward-LM project adds: (a) richer action semantics (beyond tool selection), (b) explicit VoI-based decision criteria for when to invoke tree search, and (c) evaluation on dialogue-specific failure modes (context pollution, calibration-action gap).

## Open Questions
1. **Entropy as a proxy for VoI in dialogue:** AT2PO uses policy entropy to decide when to branch, but entropy reflects model uncertainty rather than task-relevant uncertainty. For the forward-LM project, is policy entropy a good proxy for the value of information a Clarify action would yield — or does it conflate irreducible linguistic ambiguity with reducible task underspecification?

2. **Turn-wise credit assignment for repair actions:** TCA propagates terminal rewards backward assuming all turns contribute to the same terminal outcome. In dialogue with repair actions, a successful repair at turn T may **reset** the credit trajectory — the outcome after repair should not be attributed to the poor turns before it. How should TCA be modified to handle non-monotone, repairable dialogue trajectories?

3. **Scaling the tree under dialogue budget constraints:** AT2PO applies entropy-guided branching within a fixed inference budget in a retrieval-QA setting where tool calls are cheap. In real multi-turn dialogue, branching implies simulating full user responses, which is expensive. Does the entropy-guided budget allocation remain efficient when the simulation cost per branch is high — and does this suggest a natural threshold below which Clarify (branch) is preferred over Commit (single path)?
